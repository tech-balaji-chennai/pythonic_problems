Perfect Number - Problem Statement

Problem Statement:
You will be given a number 'n'. Your task is to check whether 'n' is a perfect number or not.

Example:

input 1: 
 28
output 1: 
 Yes

input 2: 
 100
output 2: 
 No
 
Definition:
Perfect number is a number if it is equal to the sum of its positive proper divisors excluding the number itself.
No need to check all positive numbers less than n for divisor of n.
Use Pad Factor's Method: To check all the numbers less than or equal to square root of number.
